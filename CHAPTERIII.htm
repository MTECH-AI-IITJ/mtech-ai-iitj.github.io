Exam Notes on Chapter III: Applications for Patents (Sections 6-11)

Section 6: Persons Entitled to Apply for Patents

  • Key Points:
    • True and First Inventor: Only the person who first invented the invention (not derived from others) can apply. Importers or those who merely discover existing inventions are excluded.
    • Assignee: A legal assignee (via assignment deed) of the inventor can apply. Proof of assignment must be submitted.
    • Legal Representative: Can apply if the inventor/assignee is deceased (e.g., executor, heir).
    • Joint Applicants: Multiple inventors or assignees can apply jointly.
    • Non-Entitled Persons: Employees (without assignment), mere discoverers, or those who reverse-engineer cannot apply.
  • Tricky Aspect: Distinguish between "first to invent" (not in India) and "first to file" (India follows this). Priority is given to the earliest filer, not the earliest inventor.

Section 7: Form of Application

  • Key Points:
    • Mandatory Forms:
      • Form 1: Application for patent grant.
      • Form 2: Provisional/complete specification.
      • Form 3: Statement/undertaking for foreign applications (if applicable).
      • Form 5: Declaration of inventorship.
      • Form 26: Power of attorney (if agent involved).
    • Filing Requirements:
      • Complete specification must be filed within 12 months of provisional specification.
      • Fees must accompany the application.
    • Tricky Aspect: Form 3 is not mandatory if no foreign applications exist. Late filing of Form 3 may lead to refusal/revocation.

Section 8: Information and Undertaking Regarding Foreign Applications

  • Key Points:
    • Requirement: Applicant must disclose details of foreign applications for the same invention.
    • Timeline:
      • If foreign application filed before Indian application: Disclose at filing.
      • If foreign application filed after Indian application: Disclose within 6 months of Indian filing.
    • Undertaking: Must update the Controller on foreign application status until grant.
    • Consequence of Non-Compliance: Application refusal or patent revocation.
  • Tricky Aspect: The 6-month deadline is from the Indian filing date, not the foreign filing date. Late disclosure risks invalidation.

Section 9: Provisional and Complete Specifications

  • Key Points:
    • Provisional Specification:
      • Establishes priority date but lacks claims.
      • Must describe the invention (not just an idea).
      • Complete specification must follow within 12 months.
    • Complete Specification:
      • Must include claims, detailed description, abstract, and drawings (if needed).
      • Must fully disclose the invention and the best method for performing it.
    • Consequence of Delay: Failure to file complete specification within 12 months abandons the application.
  • Tricky Aspect: Matter not disclosed in the provisional specification cannot claim its priority date. New matter in the complete specification gets its filing date as priority.

Section 10: Contents of Specifications

  • Key Points:
    • Complete Specification Must Include:
      1. Title (concise).
      2. Field of invention.
      3. Background art (with drawbacks).
      4. Objects of invention.
      5. Statement of invention.
      6. Summary of invention.
      7. Detailed description (enabling a person skilled in the art to perform it).
      8. Claims (defining scope).
      9. Abstract (technical summary).
      10. Drawings (if referenced).
    • Requirements:
      • Description must be sufficient (enablement) and disclose the best method.
      • Claims must be clear, concise, and supported by the description.
    • Consequence of Deficiency: Revocation for insufficiency or lack of support.
  • Tricky Aspect: "Best method" disclosure is mandatory even if alternatives exist. Omission risks revocation.

Section 11: Priority Dates of Claims

  • Key Points:
    • Priority Date Determination:
      • Claims based on provisional specification: Priority date = provisional filing date.
      • Claims based on new matter (not in provisional): Priority date = complete specification filing date.
      • Convention applications: Priority date = first foreign filing date (if claimed).
    • Divisional Applications: Retain parent application’s priority date.
    • Tricky Aspect: A claim may have multiple priority dates if it combines matter from provisional and complete specifications.

Tricky MCQs with Answers

  1. Section 6: Who can apply for a patent if the inventor dies after filing a provisional specification but before filing the complete specification?
    a) The heir of the inventor.
    b) The legal representative of the inventor.
    c) The assignee of the inventor.
    d) Both (b) and (c).
    Answer: (d). Both legal representative and assignee (if assigned) can apply.
  2. Section 7: Which form is not mandatory if no foreign applications exist?
    a) Form 1.
    b) Form 2.
    c) Form 3.
    d) Form 5.
    Answer: (c). Form 3 is required only for foreign applications.
  3. Section 8: An applicant files an Indian application on 1st January 2023 and a US application on 1st March 2023. By when must Form 3 be filed?
    a) 1st March 2023.
    b) 1st July 2023.
    c) 1st September 2023.
    d) Within 6 months of US filing.
    Answer: (b). Deadline is 6 months from Indian filing (1st July 2023), not US filing.
  4. Section 9: A provisional specification describes a chemical compound. The complete specification claims the compound and a new method to synthesize it. What is the priority date for the method claim?
    a) Provisional filing date.
    b) Complete specification filing date.
    c) Date of grant.
    d) Date of publication.
    Answer: (b). The method was not in the provisional specification.
  5. Section 10: A complete specification discloses two methods to perform an invention but omits the best method. What is the consequence?
    a) Application is rejected.
    b) Patent is revoked post-grant.
    c) Specification is amended.
    d) No consequence.
    Answer: (b). Failure to disclose the best method is grounds for revocation.
  6. Section 11: A claim in a complete specification combines matter from a provisional specification (filed 1/1/2023) and new matter added in the complete specification (filed 1/12/2023). What is the priority date?
    a) 1/1/2023 for the entire claim.
    b) 1/12/2023 for the entire claim.
    c) 1/1/2023 for provisional matter; 1/12/2023 for new matter.
    d) Date of grant.
    Answer: (c). Priority dates are split for different parts of the claim.

Tricky Use Cases with Answers

Use Case 1: Section 6 (Entitlement)

  • Scenario: Mr. X invents a device and assigns it to Mr. Y via an oral agreement. Mr. Y applies for a patent. Mr. Z claims he is the true inventor.
  • Question: Is Mr. Y entitled to the patent?
  • Answer: No. Assignments must be in writing (documentary proof). Oral agreements are invalid. Mr. Y must provide a written assignment deed.

Use Case 2: Section 8 (Foreign Applications)

  • Scenario: Applicant files an Indian application on 1/1/2023. On 1/8/2023 (after 6 months), they file a PCT application. They file Form 3 on 1/9/2023.
  • Question: Is the application valid?
  • Answer: No. Form 3 was due by 1/7/2023 (6 months from Indian filing). Late filing risks refusal unless condoned.

Use Case 3: Section 9 (Provisional Specification)

  • Scenario: A provisional specification describes a "mobile phone." The complete specification claims a "foldable mobile phone."
  • Question: Can the complete specification claim priority from the provisional?
  • Answer: No. "Foldable" is new matter not disclosed in the provisional. Priority date for this claim is the complete specification filing date.

Use Case 4: Section 10 (Best Method)

  • Scenario: An invention has two methods: Method A (inferior) and Method B (best). The specification discloses only Method A.
  • Question: Is the specification sufficient?
  • Answer: No. Failure to disclose Method B (best method) violates Section 10 and risks revocation.

Use Case 5: Section 11 (Priority Dates)

  • Scenario: Provisional specification (1/1/2023) discloses a compound. Complete specification (1/12/2023) claims the compound and a new derivative.
  • Question: What is the priority date for the derivative claim?
  • Answer: 1/12/2023. The derivative was not in the provisional specification.

Tricky Scenarios with Answers

Scenario 1: Section 6 (Joint Inventors)

  • Situation: Two researchers jointly invent a machine. One files a patent application without the other’s consent.
  • Question: Is the application valid?
  • Answer: No. Joint inventors must apply jointly. The non-consenting inventor can challenge the application.

Scenario 2: Section 7 (Late Filing of Complete Specification)

  • Situation: Provisional specification filed on 1/1/2023. Complete specification filed on 2/1/2024 (13 months later).
  • Question: What happens?
  • Answer: Application is abandoned. The 12-month deadline is strict. No extension is allowed.

Scenario 3: Section 8 (Failure to Update Foreign Status)

  • Situation: Applicant discloses a US application in Form 3. The US application is rejected, but the applicant does not inform the Indian Patent Office.
  • Question: Can the Indian patent be revoked?
  • Answer: Yes. The undertaking in Form 3 requires updating foreign status until grant. Non-disclosure is grounds for revocation.

Scenario 4: Section 10 (Insufficient Disclosure)

  • Situation: A pharmaceutical patent discloses a drug but omits critical dosage data.
  • Question: Is this sufficient?
  • Answer: No. The specification must enable a person skilled in the art to perform the invention. Missing dosage data makes it insufficient.

Scenario 5: Section 11 (Convention Priority)

  • Situation: Applicant files in the US on 1/1/2023 and in India on 1/1/2024, claiming convention priority. The Indian specification adds new claims.
  • Question: What is the priority date for the new claims?
  • Answer: 1/1/2024. Only claims disclosed in the US application get the 1/1/2023 priority date. New claims get the Indian filing date.

Exam Notes: Chapter III - Applications for Patents

Section 6: Persons entitled to apply for patents

  • Key Concept: Who can file a patent application.
  • True and First Inventor: The application must be by the "true and first inventor," their assignee, or the legal representative of either.
  • Assignee: Can be an individual, a company, or a government body. Proof of assignment (Form 1) must be submitted.
  • Joint Applicants: For joint inventors, all must apply together. If one refuses or is unavailable, the other(s) can apply on behalf of all, with proof of the situation.
  • Foreign Applicants: Must file through a registered Indian Patent Agent if they have no place of business or residence in India.

Section 7: Form of application

  • Key Concept: The procedural requirements for filing.
  • Forms: Must be filed in the prescribed Form 1 (Application) and Form 2 (Provisional/Complete Specification).
  • Fee: Must be accompanied by the prescribed fee (based on entity size: natural person, startup, small entity, or other than small entity).
  • Designation of Inventor: The name of the true and first inventor must be declared, even if the applicant is the assignee.
  • Priority Claim: If claiming priority from a convention country, details must be provided (Date, Country, Application Number).

Section 8: Information and undertaking regarding foreign applications

  • Key Concept: Duty of disclosure for corresponding foreign applications.
  • Timeline: The applicant must file a statement of undertaking (Form 3) regarding foreign applications:
    • At the time of filing: Or within three months thereof.
    • Ongoing Duty: Subsequently, within three months of filing any new application in a foreign country.
  • Information Required: Country, Application Number, Date of Filing, Status (Published, Granted, Refused, etc.).
  • Consequence of Non-Compliance: This is a crucial section. Non-furnishing of information can be a ground for opposition (pre-grant and post-grant) and even for revocation of the patent after grant.

Section 9: Provisional and complete specifications

  • Key Concept: The two-stage filing system.
  • Provisional Specification:
    • Filed when the invention is at a conceptual stage but not fully developed.
    • Must describe the invention sufficiently to establish a priority date.
    • Lower fee. Gives the applicant 12 months to further develop the invention and file the complete specification.
  • Complete Specification:
    • Must be filed within 12 months of the provisional filing date (extendable by 3 months with a fee).
    • Must fully and particularly describe the invention, including the best method of performing it, and end with claims defining the scope.
  • Conversion: A complete specification can be converted into a provisional one (within 12 months) if the applicant realizes the invention needs more work. This is rare.

Section 10: Contents of specifications

  • Key Concept: The mandatory structure and content of the specification document.
  • Title: A descriptive title of the invention.
  • Abstract: A technical summary.
  • Preamble: "The following specification particularly describes the invention and the manner in which it is to be performed."
  • Description: Must be in a logical order:
    • Field of the invention
    • Background art (Prior Art) and problems it doesn't solve
    • Object of the invention
    • Summary of the invention
    • Detailed description with drawings (if any)
    • The best method of performing the invention known to the applicant must be disclosed.
  • Claims: They define the legal scope of protection. Must be clear, concise, fully supported by the description, and relate to a single invention.

Section 11: Priority dates of claims of a complete specification

  • Key Concept: Determining the effective date of a claim for novelty and inventive step assessment.
  • General Rule: The priority date of a claim is the date of filing of the complete specification.
  • If based on a Provisional Spec: The priority date is the date of filing of the provisional specification, but only if the claim is fairly based on the matter disclosed in the provisional spec.
  • Divisional Application: The priority date is the date of the original (parent) application.
  • "Fairly Based" Test: A claim is fairly based if a skilled person would see that the claimed invention is disclosed in the provisional in a way that makes it apparent that it is part of the invention. It's a question of substance, not merely the words used.

Tricky Use Cases with Answers

Use Case 1: The Over-Enthusiastic Assignee

  • Scenario: An employee (inventor) in India develops a new process. His US-based company (the assignee) immediately files a PCT application designating the US and Europe. Six months later, they decide to file in India through their Indian subsidiary.
  • Issue: What are the Section 8 obligations for the Indian application?
  • Answer: The Indian application must be filed with a Form 3 statement at the time of filing or within 3 months. This Form 3 must disclose the details of the earlier PCT application (country, number, date). The ongoing duty requires them to update the Indian Patent Office within 3 months of any new foreign filings or significant status changes (e.g., grant in Europe).

Use Case 2: The Evolving Invention

  • Scenario: An inventor files a provisional specification for a "Device with components A and B." During the 12-month period, he makes an improvement by adding a critical "component C." The complete specification includes claims to "Device with A and B" and also to "Device with A, B, and C."
  • Issue: What are the priority dates for the two sets of claims?
  • Answer:
    • Claim for "Device with A and B": Priority date is the provisional filing date (it is fairly based on the provisional).
    • Claim for "Device with A, B, and C": Priority date is the complete specification filing date. The new element C was not disclosed in the provisional, so the claim is not fairly based on it.

Tricky Scenarios with Answers

Scenario 1: The Missing Form 3

  • Facts: Mr. X files an Indian application on 01/01/2023. He files a corresponding US application on 01/06/2023. He never files Form 3 in India for the foreign application. The Indian application is granted on 01/01/2025. A third party discovers this omission in 2026.
  • Question: What can the third party do?
  • Answer: The third party can file a petition for revocation of the patent under Section 64(1)(p) on the ground that the applicant failed to disclose information required under Section 8. The patent, even though granted, is vulnerable to revocation due to this non-compliance.

Scenario 2: The Divisional Application Priority

  • Facts: A parent application with a complete specification is filed on Jan 15, 2020. The Controller issues an objection that it claims two distinct inventions. The applicant files a divisional application for the second invention on March 10, 2021.
  • Question: What is the priority date for the claims of the divisional application?
  • Answer: The priority date for the claims in the divisional application is Jan 15, 2020 (the filing date of the parent application). This is because the divisional application is derived from the parent and its subject matter was disclosed in the parent application on that date.

Chapter III: Applications for Patents - Exam Notes 📝

Section 6: Persons Entitled to Apply for Patents

  • Who can apply? The true and first inventor, the assignee of the true and first inventor, or the legal representative of a deceased true and first inventor.
  • Key Point: An application can be filed by any of these individuals alone or jointly with any other person.
  • The True and First Inventor: The person who first invents the invention, not someone who just imports or communicates it from abroad.

Section 7: Form of Application

  • What is it? Every application for a patent must be in the prescribed form (Form 1) and must be filed at the appropriate patent office.
  • Content: The application must contain a declaration that the applicant is in possession of the invention and that they are the true and first inventor (or the assignee/legal representative).
  • Associated Documents: It must be accompanied by a provisional or complete specification, along with the prescribed fee.

Section 8: Information and Undertaking Regarding Foreign Applications

  • The Duty: An applicant must furnish a statement and an undertaking regarding any foreign applications for the same or substantially the same invention filed in any country outside India.
  • Timeline: This information must be provided within six months of filing the Indian application or within six months of the foreign application being filed, whichever is later.
  • Penalty: Failure to comply with this requirement can lead to the revocation of the granted patent. This is a very important provision for ensuring transparency.

Section 9: Provisional and Complete Specifications

  • Provisional Specification:
    • Filed to secure a priority date while the inventor is still developing the invention.
    • No claims are required, but it must describe the invention.
    • Must be followed by a complete specification within twelve months (or up to 15 months with a fee).
  • Complete Specification:
    • Must be a full and detailed description of the invention.
    • Must end with one or more claims defining the scope of the invention.
    • A single complete specification can only cover one invention or a group of inventions linked by a single inventive concept.

Section 10: Contents of Specifications

  • Provisional: Must describe the invention.
  • Complete: Must fully and particularly describe the invention and its operation or use.
    • Must disclose the best method of performing the invention.
    • Must end with at least one claim that clearly defines the scope of the invention.
    • Must be accompanied by drawings if necessary to understand the invention.
    • Must contain an abstract of the invention.
    • Crucial elements:
      • Title
      • Field of invention
      • Background
      • Summary of the invention
      • Detailed description
      • Claims
      • Abstract

Section 11: Priority Dates of Claims of a Complete Specification

  • The Rule: The priority date of a claim is the date of filing the complete specification.
  • The Exception: If a complete specification is filed after a provisional one, the priority date of a claim is the date of filing the provisional specification, provided the claim is fairly based on the matter disclosed in the provisional.
  • Multiple Priority Dates: A single complete specification can have different claims with different priority dates. For example, some claims might be based on a provisional, while others might be based on new matter added later.

Patents Act, 1970

SECTION 6: PERSONS ENTITLED TO APPLY FOR PATENTS

Key Points:

  1. True and First Inventor - Primary right holder
  2. Assignee of True and First Inventor - Can apply through assignment
  3. Legal Representative - In case of death of inventor
  4. Joint Applicants - Multiple inventors can apply together
  5. Foreign Nationals - Can apply subject to conditions

Important Provisions:

  • Application can be made by inventor or assignee
  • Assignment must be in writing and registered
  • Joint inventors have equal rights unless agreed otherwise
  • Foreign applications subject to security clearance

SECTION 7: FORM OF APPLICATION

Key Requirements:

  1. Prescribed Form - Form 1 for ordinary applications
  2. Fee Payment - Prescribed fee must be paid
  3. Specification - Complete or provisional
  4. Drawings (if any) - When necessary for understanding
  5. Priority Documents - For convention applications

Types of Applications:

  • Ordinary Application - Direct filing in India
  • Convention Application - Based on foreign priority

🔹 Section 6: Persons entitled to apply for patents

"An application for a patent for an invention may be made by any person claiming to be the true and first inventor of the invention..."

Key Points:

  • True and first inventor: The actual creator of the invention.
  • Legal representative: If the inventor is deceased, their legal heir/representative can apply.
  • Assignee: An applicant may assign rights before filing; the assignee can then apply.
  • Joint applicants: Two or more persons can jointly apply if they are joint inventors or co-owners.
  • Government: Can apply if it acquires rights from the inventor.

📌 Note: The term "person" includes individuals, companies, firms, and legal entities.

⚠️ Trick: Even if someone else files first, only the true and first inventor (or their assignee) has the right to apply. A thief cannot file validly.

🔹 Section 7: Form of application

Every application must be filed in the prescribed form (Form 1), with provisional/complete specification (Form 2), along with fees.

Requirements:

  • Filed at the appropriate Patent Office (based on jurisdiction).
  • Must include:
    • Form 1: Application for grant of patent
    • Form 2: Specification (provisional or complete)
    • Form 3: Statement and undertaking under Section 8 (foreign filings)
    • Form 5: Declaration of inventorship (if complete spec)
    • Form 18 or 18A: Request for examination (within 48 months)
  • Filed electronically or physically.

📌 Note: Filing without specification (Form 2) leads to abandonment unless submitted within 12 months (for provisional).

🔹 Section 8: Information and undertaking regarding foreign applications

Applicant must disclose details of corresponding foreign applications and update the Controller until grant.

Obligations:

  • Within 6 months of filing in India, submit:
    • Details of each foreign application (country, filing date, serial no., status).
    • Provide updates whenever prosecution events occur abroad (e.g., allowance, rejection, amendments).
  • Failure to comply → Application deemed abandoned (Section 8(2)).

📌 Critical Point: Applies even if foreign filing happened after Indian filing. 📌 Exception: Not required for PCT national phase entry if international bureau transmitted data.

⚠️ Trick: Duty is ongoing until grant — not a one-time requirement.

🔹 Section 9: Provisional and complete specifications

An applicant may file a provisional specification to establish priority, followed by complete specification within 12 months.

Types:

Provisional

Disclose concept early; secure priority date

Must be followed bycomplete spec within 12 months

Complete

Full disclosure; enables examination

Can be filed directly (no provisional needed)

📌 Note: No invention can be patented based solely on provisional spec. 📌 Extension? No extension beyond 12 months — lapse = abandonment.

🔹 Section 10: Contents of specifications

Complete specification must fully describe the invention and end with claims defining the scope.

Mandatory Parts:

  1. Title
  2. Field of invention
  3. Background (prior art, problems)
  4. Summary of invention
  5. Detailed description (with examples)
  6. Drawings (if any)
  7. Claims (most important part – defines legal protection)
  8. Abstract

📌 Enablement Requirement: Must enable a POSITA (Person Skilled in the Art) to perform the invention.

📌 Best Mode: While not explicitly required in Indian law, full disclosure is expected.

⚠️ Trick: Claims must be clear, concise, and supported by the description.

🔹 Section 11: Priority dates of claims of a complete specification

If complete spec is filed within 12 months of provisional, all claims get the priority date of the provisional, provided they are fairly based on it.

Conditions:

  • Claim must be supported in the provisional spec.
  • If new matter is added in complete spec → that claim gets date of complete spec.
  • Multiple priorities allowed (e.g., via Paris Convention or PCT).

📌 Example: Provisional discloses a phone with camera; complete adds fingerprint sensor → sensor claim gets later date.

📌 PCT Entry: National phase entry in India allows claiming priority date of PCT/international filing.

Exam Notes on Chapter III: Applications for Patents

These notes cover the key provisions of Sections 6 to 11 from The Patents Act, 1970 (as amended). They focus on essential concepts, requirements, and implications for patent applications in India. I've structured them section-wise for clarity, highlighting important subsections and practical points relevant for exams.

Section 6: Persons Entitled to Apply for Patents

  • This section defines who can file a patent application, emphasizing the principle of true and first inventorship.
  • Key provisions:
    • (1) Applications can be made by: (a) the true and first inventor; (b) an assignee of the true and first inventor; (c) the legal representative of a deceased person entitled to apply.
    • Subject to Section 134 (which deals with assignments and transmissions).
    • (2) Applications can be filed alone or jointly with others.
  • Important notes: "True and first inventor" excludes importers or communicators of inventions from abroad. Joint applications require all parties' consent. This section ensures only legitimate claimants apply, preventing fraudulent filings.

Section 7: Form of Application

  • Outlines the formal requirements for filing a patent application to ensure standardization and validity.
  • Key provisions:
    • (1) Every application must be for one invention only, in the prescribed form, and filed at the patent office.
    • (1A) International applications under the Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT) designating India are deemed applications under this Act if a corresponding national application is filed.
    • (1B) The filing date for PCT applications processed in India is the international filing date.
    • (2) If filed by an assignee, proof of right (e.g., assignment deed) must be submitted with the application or within the prescribed period.
    • (3) The application must state possession of the invention, name the true and first inventor, and include a declaration if the inventor is not the applicant.
    • (4) Non-convention or non-PCT applications must be accompanied by a provisional or complete specification.
  • Important notes: Form 1 is typically used. Failure to provide proof of right can lead to abandonment. This section links to Form 2 for specifications.

Section 8: Information and Undertaking Regarding Foreign Applications

  • Requires disclosure of foreign filings to promote transparency and assist in examination for novelty.
  • Key provisions:
    • (1) If prosecuting the same or substantially similar invention abroad (alone or jointly), the applicant must file: (a) detailed particulars of foreign applications; (b) an undertaking to inform the Controller of subsequent foreign filings up to grant in India. This must be done with the application or within the prescribed period.
    • (2) The Controller can require details on foreign processing anytime before grant, and the applicant must provide them within the prescribed time.
  • Important notes: Form 3 is used for statements. Non-compliance can lead to opposition or revocation under Section 25 or 64. Prescribed period is usually 6 months, extendable.

Section 9: Provisional and Complete Specifications

  • Distinguishes between provisional and complete specifications, allowing time for refinement while securing priority.
  • Key provisions:
    • (1) If accompanied by provisional specification (non-convention/PCT), complete specification must be filed within 12 months, or the application is abandoned.
    • (2) For cognate inventions or modifications, the Controller may allow one complete specification for multiple provisionals, with priority from the earliest.
    • (3) If filed with a purported complete specification, the applicant can request it be treated as provisional within 12 months.
    • (4) After filing complete in pursuance of provisional, the applicant can request cancellation of provisional and post-dating to the complete filing date (before grant).
  • Important notes: Provisional protects priority without full disclosure. No claims in provisional. Extension of 12 months not allowed; abandonment is automatic.

Section 10: Contents of Specifications

  • Specifies the structure and content of specifications to ensure clarity, sufficiency, and enablement.
  • Key provisions:
    • (1) Must describe the invention with a title indicating the subject matter.
    • (2) Drawings may be supplied and form part of the specification unless directed otherwise.
    • (3) Models/samples may be required but don't form part of the specification.
    • (4) Complete specification must: (a) fully describe the invention, operation, and method; (b) disclose the best known method; (c) end with claims defining scope; (d) include an abstract.
      • Proviso: Controller can amend abstract. For biological material, deposit under Budapest Treaty if not describable/available, with details in specification (e.g., deposit date, institution).
    • (4A) For PCT applications designating India, the international filing documents serve as complete specification.
    • (5) Claims must relate to one invention or linked group, be clear, succinct, and based on disclosure.
    • (6) Declaration of inventorship (Form 5) in prescribed cases.
    • (7) Complete after provisional can include developments/additions eligible for separate application under Section 6.
  • Important notes: Sufficiency is key—must enable skilled person to perform invention. Abstract is 150 words max. Biological deposits must be before filing date.

Section 11: Priority Dates of Claims of a Complete Specification

  • Establishes priority dates for claims, crucial for novelty and opposition.
  • Key provisions:
    • (1) Each claim has a priority date.
    • (2) For complete filed after provisional (or treated as such), priority is provisional filing date if claim fairly based on it.
    • (3) For multiple provisionals, priority from the relevant or later one if based partly on each.
    • (3A) For complete based on prior Indian application within 12 months, priority from prior if fairly based.
    • (4) For divisional applications (Section 16), priority from earlier specification if fairly based.
    • (5) If multiple priorities, earliest applies.
    • (6) Otherwise, priority is complete filing date (subject to Section 137 for conventions).
    • (7) References to filing dates account for post-dating (Sections 9/17) or ante-dating (Section 16).
    • (8) Claims not invalid due to post-priority publication/use or grant of another patent with same/later priority.
  • Important notes: "Fairly based" means substantial disclosure in earlier document. Priority determines what counts as prior art.

Section 6: Persons Entitled to Apply for Patents

  • Only the true and first inventor, assignee of the inventor, or legal representative of a deceased inventor can apply.
  • Application can be made alone or jointly.
  • The "true and first inventor" excludes first importers and those only communicated about inventions from abroad.
  • Assignees (company, employer) need a valid assignment deed.
  • Legal representative applies only if the original right-holder dies before applying.

Section 7: Form of Application

  • Application must be for one invention only, made in the prescribed form (Form 1) and filed at the patent office.
  • International PCT applications designating India are deemed Indian applications if filed with the Controller.
  • Applications by assignment must prove the right to apply (assignment deed).
  • Application must name the true and first inventor and state possession of invention.
  • Must be accompanied by either a provisional or complete specification (unless PCT or convention application).

Section 8: Information and Undertaking Regarding Foreign Applications

  • Applicant must disclose details of foreign applications relating to the same invention at the time of filing or within prescribed time. Includes applications filed or known by the applicant or their title holder.
  • Must undertake to keep the Controller informed of such applications until grant.
  • Controller can require further information (status, prosecution details, etc.).
  • Non-disclosure may result in refusal or revocation of Indian patent.

Section 9: Provisional and Complete Specifications

  • Application may be filed with a provisional or complete specification.
  • If provisional, complete specification must be filed within 12 months, else application is abandoned.
  • Provisional secures a priority date.
  • Complete specification required before grant and must fully describe the invention.

Section 10: Contents of Specifications

  • Specification must:
    • Begin with a title, describe invention (technical field, prior art, solution, advantages).
    • Fully and particularly describe the invention and best method (enablement).
    • Disclose any biological material/source/geographical origin as required.
    • End with claims defining scope of protection.
    • Be clear, concise, and supported with drawings if necessary.

Section 11: Priority Dates of Claims

  • Priority date is the date of filing the specification for each claim unless otherwise claimed (e.g., for amended/divisional applications).
  • For claims based on provisional, priority is the provisional’s date if substance is disclosed therein.
  • Convention applications may claim earliest priority date from a foreign application.

Chapter III: Applications for Patents (Sections 6-11)

Section 6: Persons entitled to apply for patents

  • Who can apply: The true and first inventor, the assignee of the true and first inventor, or the legal representative of a deceased person who was entitled to apply.
  • Alone or jointly: Application can be made by any of these persons either alone or jointly with others.
  • Proof of right: If an assignee files, they must submit proof of their right to file and a declaration that the inventor is the first and true inventor.
  • "True and first inventor": This term doesn't include the first importer of an invention or someone to whom an invention is communicated outside India.

Section 7: Form of application

  • Single invention: Each application must be for one invention only, or a group of inventions linked by a single inventive concept.
  • Prescribed form and filing: The application must be filed in the prescribed form at the appropriate patent office.
  • PCT applications: International applications under the Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT) designating India are considered applications under this Act if a corresponding application is also filed in India.

Section 8: Information and undertaking regarding foreign applications

  • Disclosure requirement: Applicants must provide details of applications filed in other countries for the same or substantially the same invention.
  • Form 3 and undertaking: This is done by filing a statement in Form 3 along with the application (or later within the prescribed period). The applicant must also undertake to keep the Controller informed of any further applications or details until the grant of the Indian patent.
  • Consequences of non-compliance: Failure to comply with Section 8 can lead to rejection of the application, revocation of the patent, and even refusal of an interim injunction in infringement proceedings.

Section 9: Provisional and complete specifications

  • A provisional specification can be filed to secure a priority date.
  • A complete specification must be filed within 12 months from the provisional filing date; otherwise, the application is deemed abandoned.
  • Multiple provisional applications for related inventions may be combined into a single complete specification.
  • A complete specification can be treated as provisional under certain conditions and within a 12-month period.
  • Before patent grant, an applicant can request to cancel the provisional specification and post-date the application to the complete specification filing date.

Section 10: Contents of specifications

  • Both provisional and complete specifications require a title and description of the invention.
  • Drawings can be included and are part of the specification unless directed otherwise. Models or samples required by the Controller are not part of the specification.
  • A complete specification must fully describe the invention, including the best method of performance. It must conclude with clear, succinct claims based on the description, defining the scope of protection. An abstract providing technical information is also required. Special conditions apply for inventions involving unavailable biological material.

Section 11: Priority dates of claims of a complete specification

  • Each claim in a complete specification has a priority date. This is generally the filing date of the specification (provisional or complete) where the claim's subject matter was first disclosed, provided the claim is fairly based on that disclosure.
  • If a complete specification is based on multiple earlier specifications, the priority date is determined by the filing date(s) of the relevant specification(s). If a claim is based partly on one and partly on another, the priority date is the later date.
  • The earliest applicable date under these rules is the priority date. In other cases, the priority date is the complete specification filing date, subject to Section 137.

Tricky Use Cases with Answers

Use Case 1: Complex Inventorship
Company X develops a novel chemical process. Dr. Ali conceptualized the core idea, Dr. Ben refined the process, and Dr. Clara discovered a critical catalyst that significantly improved its efficiency. The company wants to file a patent application.

Question: Who should be listed as the inventor(s) in the patent application under Section 6?

Answer: Dr. Ali, Dr. Ben, and Dr. Clara should all be listed as joint inventors. This is because Section 6 allows the true and first inventor(s) to apply, either alone or jointly. Inventorship extends to anyone who contributed to the inventive step.

Use Case 2: Post-Dating Application
An applicant files a provisional application on February 10, 2025, for a new type of battery. They file the complete specification on January 10, 2026. However, they realize that a significant improvement was made to the battery on December 1, 2025, which they want to be covered by the patent.

Question: What action can the applicant take regarding the application date and the scope of claims?

Answer: The applicant can request to cancel the provisional specification and post-date the application to January 10, 2026 (the complete specification filing date) to potentially cover the December 1, 2025 improvement. Under Section 9(4), post-dating is permitted before the patent grant. Additionally, Section 10(7) allows including claims for developments made after the provisional specification in the complete specification, provided a separate application for them would have been permissible under Section 6.

Tricky Scenarios with Answers

Scenario 1: Incomplete disclosure in foreign applications

An Indian applicant files a patent application in India, disclosing a novel chemical compound. They also have a corresponding application in the US, but the US application initially disclosed a broader class of compounds and only later amended it to include the specific novel compound disclosed in India. The Indian application’s statement under Section 8(1)(a) accurately reflects the initial US application (the broader class of compounds).

Question: Has the applicant fully complied with Section 8? What are the potential consequences?

Answer: No, the applicant has not fully complied. They are required under Section 8(1)(b) to provide an undertaking to keep the Controller informed of all subsequent foreign applications or developments related to the same or substantially the same invention. The amendment in the US application concerning the specific novel compound should have been disclosed. Failure to disclose information under Section 8 can lead to opposition, revocation, or affect interim injunctions in infringement cases.

Scenario 2: Clarity and Support of Claims

An applicant files a complete specification for a new software algorithm. The claims drafted are very broad, potentially covering various functionalities that are not clearly and distinctly explained in the detailed description provided in the specification.

Question: What are the potential issues with this application under Section 10 and how might the Controller react?

Answer: The application is likely to face objections under Section 10(5). This section requires claims to be clear, succinct, and fairly based on the matter disclosed in the specification. If the description does not adequately support the broad claims, the Controller may object and require amendments to narrow the claims or provide further supporting disclosure.

1) Exam Notes (laser‑focused on §§6–11)

§6 — Persons entitled to apply

  • Who may apply: (i) the true & first inventor, (ii) the assignee of the true & first inventor (proof of right needed), or (iii) the legal representative of a deceased person who was so entitled; any of the above alone or jointly.ipindia+1
  • Tip for exam traps: “True & first inventor” excludes the first importer or first communicator of an invention from abroad. (Concept routinely tested via negative MCQs.) Back up: stick to §6 language.ipindia

§7 — Form of application

  • One invention per application; file in the prescribed form (Form 1) at the Patent Office.ipindia
  • PCT designating India: an international application designating India is deemed an application under the Act if a corresponding application is filed before the Controller in India (i.e., you must enter national phase). The filing date is the international filing date.ipindia
  • Proof of right (if by assignee) may be furnished with the application or within the prescribed period. Name the true & first inventor and add the belief declaration if the applicant is not the inventor. Applications (other than convention/PCT) must be accompanied by provisional or complete specification (Form 2).ipindia
  • Forms you’ll touch:Form 1 (application), Form 2 (provisional/complete), Form 5 (declaration as to inventorship, where applicable).ipindia

§8 — Information & undertaking re. foreign applications

  • Core duty: File a statement & undertaking (Form 3) listing corresponding foreign filings and undertake to keep the Controller updated until grant.ipindia+1
  • Deadlines under Rule 12 (as amended in 2024):
    • Initial Form 3:within 6 months from the Indian filing date (for PCT, 6 months from the actual Indian national phase filing).ipindia
    • Subsequent updates: within 3 months from the date of issuance of the First Examination Report (FER)/first statement of objections (Rule 24B(3)/24C(8)). (This replaced the old rolling “within 6 months of each foreign filing” burden.)ipindia
    • Controller’s requisition under §8(2): supply processing details within 2 months of the communication; Controller may use accessible databases too. Delay condonable up to 3 months via Form 4.ipindia
  • Exam favorite: Non‑compliance with §8 can ground revocation under §64(1)(m) (commonly cited in case‑law discussions). (See legal analyses summarizing practice.)khuranaandkhurana+1

§9 — Provisional and complete specifications

  • If you file provisional, you must file complete within 12 months from the provisional filing date; otherwise deemed abandoned.ipindia
  • Multiple provisionals for cognate/modification inventions can roll into one complete, if the Controller agrees they form a single invention; the 12‑month clock runs from the earliest provisional.ipindia
  • A purported complete can be treated as provisional (on request) within 12 months of filing.ipindia
  • Post‑dating to complete date is possible (on request before grant) by cancelling the provisional; this interacts with §17 post‑dating limits (max 6 months).ipindia+1

§10 — Contents of specifications

  • All specs (provisional or complete) must describe the invention and begin with a title; drawings may form part of the spec if required.ipindia
  • Complete specification must: (a) fully and particularly describe the invention & how to perform it; (b) disclose the best method known to the applicant; (c) end with claims; and (d) include an abstract (Controller may amend the abstract for third‑party clarity).ipindia
  • Biological material: If not sufficiently describable and not publicly available, deposit under Budapest Treaty (by filing date) and disclose depository detailsand the source & geographical origin of the biological material in the spec.ipindia
  • PCT national phase: the PCT title/description/drawings/abstract/claims are taken as the complete specification in India (§10(4A)).ipindia

§11 — Priority dates of claims (claim‑by‑claim!)

  • Every claim has its own priority date.ipindia
  • Fairly based on provisional → claim gets provisional filing date; with multiple provisionals, a claim based partly in one and partly in another gets the later date; if the claim would have two or more dates, take the earliest that fully supports the claim.ipindia
  • 12‑month “internal priority” (§11(3A)): if a complete spec is filed within 12 months from an earlier Indian application, claims fairly based get the earlier filing date.ipindia
  • Divisionals (§16) impact: if a claim is fairly based on an earlier spec (provisional/complete), you keep the first disclosure date. Post‑/ante‑dating under §§9/16/17 flows through §11(7).ipindia
