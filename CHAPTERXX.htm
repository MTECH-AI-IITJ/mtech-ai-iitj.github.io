Exam Notes on Chapter XX: Penalties (Sections 118–124)

Section 118: Contravention of Secrecy Provisions

  • Purpose: Penalizes unauthorized disclosure of inventions deemed relevant to defense/atomic energy under Section 35.
  • Key Elements:
    • Applies to any person notified under Section 35 (e.g., inventors, assignees).
    • Disclosure must be in contravention of secrecy directions.
  • Penalty: Imprisonment up to 2 years, fine, or both.
  • Note: No penalty if disclosure is authorized by the Central Government.

Section 119: Falsification of Entries in Register

  • Purpose: Prevents fraud in patent records/documents.
  • Key Elements:
    • Making false entries in the patent register.
    • Producing/tendering false documents (e.g., forged patents).
    • False representations to obtain register entries.
  • Penalty: Imprisonment up to 2 years, fine, or both.

Section 120: Unauthorized Claim of Patent Rights

  • Purpose: Penalizes false representations about patent status.
  • Key Elements:
    • Using "patented," "patent applied for," etc., when no patent exists.
    • Falsely claiming an article/process is patented.
  • Penalty: Fine up to ₹500 (no imprisonment).
  • Note: Applies to articles/products only, not services.

Section 121: Wrongful Use of "Patent Office"

  • Purpose: Prevents misuse of government authority.
  • Key Elements:
    • Unauthorized use of "Patent Office" or similar terms implying government affiliation.
  • Penalty: Fine up to ₹500.

Section 122: Refusal/Failure to Supply Information

  • Purpose: Ensures compliance with statutory information requests.
  • Key Elements:
    • Refusal/failure to provide information to the Controller/Central Government without reasonable cause.
  • Penalty: Imprisonment up to 6 months, fine, or both.

Section 123: Practice by Non-Registered Patent Agents

  • Purpose: Regulates patent agent practice.
  • Key Elements:
    • Acting as a patent agent without registration.
    • Using titles like "Patent Agent" when unregistered.
  • Penalty: Imprisonment up to 6 months, fine up to ₹500, or both.
  • Note: Drafting specifications alone does not constitute "practice" if no representation before the Controller occurs.

Section 124: Offences by Companies

  • Purpose: Holds companies and responsible personnel accountable.
  • Key Elements:
    • Offences committed by companies: Liability extends to directors/managers if they consented/neglected due diligence.
    • Defense: Prove offence occurred without knowledge or due diligence was exercised.
  • Penalty: Company fined; individuals face imprisonment/fine as per the specific offence.

Exam Notes on Penalties (Chapter XX of The Patents Act, 1970)

This section of the Patents Act deals with offenses and punishments related to the misuse of patent rights, falsification of records, unauthorized practice, and failure to comply with certain obligations. It's crucial to understand the specific acts that constitute an offense and the corresponding penalties.

Section 118. Contravention of secrecy provisions relating to certain inventions

This section applies to inventions that are subject to secrecy directions from the Controller under Section 35, or those filed outside India in contravention of Section 39.

  • Offense: Failure to comply with a secrecy direction or filing a patent application in a foreign country without obtaining prior permission as required.
  • Penalty: Imprisonment for up to two years, or with a fine, or with both.

Section 119. Falsification of entries in register, etc.

This section prohibits the falsification of official patent records.

  • Offense:
    • Making a false entry in the Register of Patents.
    • Creating a document that falsely claims to be a copy of an entry from the register.
    • Knowingly producing or using such a false document as evidence.
  • Penalty: This is a serious offense, and the section states the person shall be punishable, though the specific penalty is not detailed in the section itself. It's treated as a criminal offense under general law.

Section 120. Unauthorised claim of patent rights

This section deals with the false representation of a product as patented or having a patent pending.

  • Offense: Falsely representing that an article sold is patented in India or is the subject of a pending patent application.
  • How it's represented: The use of words like "patent," "patented," "patent applied for," "patent pending" or any other words implying that a patent exists or has been applied for, on the article itself or its packaging.
  • Penalty: A fine which may extend to one lakh rupees (₹1,00,000).

Section 121. Wrongful use of words "patent office"

This section prevents individuals from misusing the name of the Patent Office to mislead the public.

  • Offense: Using the words "patent office" or any similar words on a place of business or any document that would lead someone to believe the place is or is officially connected with the Indian Patent Office.
  • Penalty: Imprisonment for a term that may extend to six months, or with a fine, or with both.

Section 122. Refusal or failure to supply information

This section penalizes the non-compliance with requests for information from the government or the Controller.

  • Offense:
    • Refusing or failing to provide information to the Central Government as required by Section 100(5) (related to patented inventions used for government purposes).
    • Refusing or failing to provide information or a statement to the Controller as required by Section 146 (related to the working of a patented invention).
  • Penalty:
    • For failure to furnish information: A fine that may extend to ten lakh rupees (₹10,00,000).
    • For furnishing false information: Imprisonment for a term which may extend to six months, or with a fine, or with both.

Section 123. Practice by non-registered patent agents

This section is crucial for maintaining the integrity of the patent agent profession.

  • Offense: A person who is not a registered patent agent practices as one, in contravention of Section 129.
  • Penalty:
    • First offense: A fine which may extend to one lakh rupees (₹1,00,000).
    • Second or subsequent offense: A fine which may extend to five lakh rupees (₹5,00,000).

Section 124. Offences by companies

This section clarifies the liability when an offense under the Act is committed by a company.

  • Liability: The company itself, as well as every person in charge of and responsible for the company's business at the time the offense was committed, are considered guilty.
  • Defense: A person can be acquitted if they can prove that the offense was committed without their knowledge or that they exercised all due diligence to prevent it.
  • Additional Liability: If the offense was committed with the consent or connivance of a director, manager, or other officer, or is due to their neglect, they will also be held guilty.

CHAPTER XX - PENALTIES: Complete Exam Notes

SECTION 118: CONTRAVENTION OF SECRECY PROVISIONS RELATING TO CERTAIN INVENTIONS

Key Points:

  • Purpose: Protects national security by maintaining secrecy of certain inventions
  • Application: Applies to inventions that may be prejudicial to defense of India or safety/interests of the state
  • Penalty: Imprisonment up to 2 years OR fine OR both
  • Scope: Covers both Indian nationals and foreign nationals

Important Provisions:

  1. Secrecy Direction: Controller may issue directions to treat certain applications as secret
  2. Publication Restrictions: No publication of such applications without authorization
  3. Communication Restrictions: No communication of information to unauthorized persons
  4. International Filing: Restrictions on filing applications abroad for such inventions

Tricky Points:

  • Penalty applies even if person didn't know the invention was classified as secret
  • Covers not just inventors but anyone who gains access to secret information
  • No defense of "good faith" or "lack of knowledge"

SECTION 119: FALSIFICATION OF ENTRIES IN REGISTER, ETC.

Key Points:

  • Target: Prevents tampering with official patent records
  • Penalty: Imprisonment up to 3 years OR fine OR both
  • Scope: Covers registers, documents, and certificates

Covered Activities:

  1. Making false entries in any register
  2. Making writing falsely purporting to be a copy of entry
  3. Producing false copies as genuine
  4. Willfully making false statements for register entries

Important Distinctions:

  • Intent Required: "Willfully" or "knowingly" - mens rea is essential
  • Official Documents: Only applies to official patent office documents
  • Authentication: Includes false certificates and authenticated copies

SECTION 120: UNAUTHORISED CLAIM OF PATENT RIGHTS

Key Points:

  • Purpose: Prevents false claims of patent protection
  • Penalty: Fine up to ₹1,00,000
  • Nature: Summary offense, relatively minor penalty

Prohibited Acts:

  1. False Marking: Using "patent," "patented," or similar words on unpatented articles
  2. Expired Patents: Continuing to mark articles after patent expiry
  3. Misleading Public: Creating impression of patent protection when none exists

Defenses Available:

  • Innocent Infringement: If defendant proves he didn't know and had no reasonable means of knowing
  • Good Faith: Genuine belief that article was patented

Practical Examples:

  • Marking "Patent Pending" when no application filed
  • Using "Patented in India" on imported goods patented only abroad
  • Continuing patent markings after patent lapses

SECTION 121: WRONGFUL USE OF WORDS "PATENT OFFICE"

Key Points:

  • Purpose: Protects official designation and prevents confusion
  • Penalty: Fine up to ₹10,000
  • Scope: Use of "Patent Office" or words suggesting official connection

Prohibited Uses:

  1. Business Names: Using "Patent Office" in business/company names
  2. Misleading Titles: Words suggesting connection with official patent office
  3. Professional Services: Patent agents/attorneys using confusing terminology

Exceptions:

  • Authorized Use: With permission from Central Government
  • Descriptive Use: Genuine reference to the official patent office
  • Educational Use: Academic or informational references

SECTION 122: REFUSAL OR FAILURE TO SUPPLY INFORMATION

Key Points:

  • Purpose: Ensures compliance with information requests from Controller
  • Penalty: Fine up to ₹10,000
  • Application: When Controller requires information under the Act

Covered Situations:

  1. Compulsory Licensing: Information required for CL applications
  2. Working Requirements: Details about patent working
  3. Opposition Proceedings: Relevant information for opposition
  4. Revocation Proceedings: Information supporting revocation

Important Notes:

  • Reasonable Excuse: Defense available for legitimate reasons
  • Scope of Information: Must be within Controller's statutory powers
  • Time Limits: Usually specified time limits for compliance

SECTION 123: PRACTICE BY NON-REGISTERED PATENT AGENTS

Key Points:

  • Purpose: Regulates patent agency profession
  • Penalty: Fine up to ₹1,00,000 for each offense
  • Scope: Restricts who can practice as patent agent

Prohibited Activities:

  1. Representation: Acting as patent agent without registration
  2. Preparation: Preparing patent documents for fees
  3. Advice Services: Providing patent advice professionally
  4. Business Operations: Operating patent agency business

Exceptions:

  1. Personal Applications: Individual filing own application
  2. Employer-Employee: Employee acting for employer
  3. Legal Practitioners: Advocates in certain circumstances
  4. Foreign Agents: In specific international situations

Registration Requirements:

  • Examination: Must pass patent agent examination
  • Eligibility: Technical qualifications required
  • Maintenance: Continuous registration maintenance

  2. Director Liability: Every person in charge deemed guilty
  3. Officer Liability: Managers, secretaries, and other officers included

✅ EXAM NOTES

🔹 Section 118: Contravention of Secrecy Provisions Relating to Certain Inventions

  • Applies to inventions relevant to defence or atomic energy.
  • If an invention is subject to secrecy directions under Section 35, the applicant must not publish or disclose it without prior permission from the Controller.
  • Penalty: Up to 2 years imprisonment or fine, or both.
  • Even applying for a patent abroad without prior permission can attract this penalty.
  • Applies even if the patent is ultimately refused — secrecy obligation remains.

Key Point: This section ensures national security; disclosure = criminal offence.

🔹 Section 119: Falsification of Entries in Register, etc.

  • It is an offence to falsify, tamper with, or make false entries in the Register of Patents, journals, or any official document maintained by the Patent Office.
  • Also includes making false representations about such documents.
  • Penalty: Up to 2 years imprisonment, or fine, or both.

Note: The Register is a public record, but its integrity must be preserved.

🔹 Section 120: Unauthorised Claim of Patent Rights

  • It is illegal to falsely represent that an article is patented when it is not.
  • Includes using terms like “patented”, “patent pending”, “patent applied for” without valid basis.
  • Applies even if the application was filed but later abandoned.
  • Penalty: Up to Rs. 1 lakh fine (no imprisonment).
  • Exception: If the person proves they had reasonable grounds to believe it was patented, no penalty.

Trick: Mere filing ≠ right to mark as "patent pending" indefinitely.

🔹 Section 121: Wrongful Use of Words "Patent Office"

  • No person shall use the name “Patent Office” or any name likely to be mistaken for it (e.g., “National Patent Bureau”) in connection with any business unless authorised.
  • Prevents misrepresentation that a private entity is a government body.
  • Penalty: Up to Rs. 1 lakh fine.

Note: Does not include imprisonment — only fine.

🔹 Section 122: Refusal or Failure to Supply Information

  • Controller may require any person to furnish information or produce documents.
  • If a person refuses or fails to comply without reasonable cause, they commit an offence.
  • Penalty: Up to Rs. 10,000 fine.
  • Applies to applicants, agents, opponents, third parties, etc.

Important: Reasonable cause (e.g., privilege, confidentiality) may be a defence.

🔹 Section 123: Practice by Non-Registered Patent Agents

  • Only registered patent agents can practice before the Controller.
  • Non-registered persons shall not:
    • Prepare patent specifications,
    • Conduct prosecution,
    • Advise on patent matters for fee/consideration.
  • Penalty: Up to Rs. 1 lakh fine for each offence.
  • Note: A lawyer (advocate) advising generally on IP law is not violating this — only if acting as a patent agent.

Grey Area: Can a non-agent help file a patent? → Technically, an inventor can file alone, but professional representation requires registration.

🔹 Section 124: Offences by Companies

  • If an offence under the Act is committed by a company, every person in charge of and responsible for conduct (e.g., directors, managers, secretaries) shall be deemed guilty.
  • Exception: If the person proves the offence was committed without their knowledge or that they exercised all due diligence, they may escape liability.
  • Company itself is liable, along with responsible individuals.

Key Point: Vicarious liability applies unless due diligence is proven.

Exam Notes on Chapter XX: Penalties (The Patents Act, 1970, as amended up to the Jan Vishwas (Amendment of Provisions) Act, 2023)

Chapter XX of the Patents Act, 1970 (Sections 118-124) outlines penalties for various contraventions related to patents, aiming to deter misuse, falsification, and non-compliance. The Jan Vishwas Act, 2023 (effective 2024) decriminalized several offences by replacing imprisonment with monetary penalties in some sections, introduced adjudicating officers for inquiries, and added appeal mechanisms. It also provides for a 10% increase in minimum fines/penalties every 3 years from the commencement date. Note that penalties are now often handled administratively via an adjudicating officer (Section 124A), with appeals to a higher authority (Section 124B). Non-compliance with orders can lead to additional fine of ₹1 lakh or imprisonment up to 1 year, or both.

Key points for each section:

  • Section 118: Contravention of secrecy provisions relating to certain inventions
    • Applies to failure to comply with directions under Section 35 (secrecy directions for inventions relevant to defense) or filing a patent application abroad without permission under Section 39.
    • Penalty: Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both (no change from Jan Vishwas Act).
    • Key: Focuses on national security; "certain inventions" typically involve defense or atomic energy. No decriminalization here due to sensitivity.
  • Section 119: Falsification of entries in register, etc.
    • Covers making false entries in the patent register, creating false copies, or producing/tendering false evidence knowing it to be false.
    • Penalty: Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both (no change).
    • Key: Protects the integrity of the patent registry; criminal intent (knowledge of falsity) is essential.
  • Section 120: Unauthorised claim of patent rights
    • Prohibits false representation that an article is patented in India or subject to a pending patent application.
    • Penalty (amended by Jan Vishwas Act): Penalty up to ₹10 lakh; for continuing claims, additional ₹1,000 per day.
    • Explanation 1: Deemed representation if words like "patent," "patented," "patent applied for," or "patent pending" are used on the article.
    • Explanation 2: Such words imply reference to Indian patents unless clearly indicated as foreign.
    • Key: Targets false advertising; now fully decriminalized (no imprisonment), with compounding via adjudicating officer.
  • Section 121: Wrongful use of words "patent office"
    • Prohibited use of "patent office" or similar words implying official connection on business premises or documents.
    • Amendment by Jan Vishwas Act: Section omitted entirely (no penalty now; decriminalized and removed).
  • Section 122: Refusal or failure to supply information
    • Sub-section (1): Failure to furnish information to Central Government under Section 100(5) (working of patents) or to Controller under Section 146 (statement of working).
      • Penalty (amended): Up to ₹1 lakh; for continuing refusal, additional ₹1,000 per day.
    • Sub-section (2): Furnishing false information knowingly.
      • Penalty (amended): Sum equal to 0.5% of total sales/turnover/gross receipts (from audited accounts) or ₹5 crore, whichever is less.
    • Key: Promotes transparency in patent working; fully decriminalized (imprisonment removed from sub-section 2).
  • Section 123: Practice by non-registered patent agents
    • Contravention of Section 129 (restrictions on unregistered agents practicing before Controller).
    • Penalty (amended): Up to ₹5 lakh; for continuing default, additional ₹1,000 per day.
    • Key: Ensures professional standards; first/second offence distinction removed, now uniform with continuing penalty.
  • Section 124: Offences by companies
    • Holds company and responsible persons (e.g., directors, managers) liable for offences.
    • Defense: Prove offence committed without knowledge or due diligence exercised.
    • Penalty: As per the specific offence (no direct change).
    • Key: Vicarious liability; consent/connivance/neglect makes officers personally liable.
  • New Section 124A: Adjudication of penalties (inserted by Jan Vishwas Act)
    • Controller authorizes an officer (under Section 73) as adjudicating officer to inquire and impose penalties after hearing the party.
    • Key: Administrative process for efficiency.
  • New Section 124B: Appeal (inserted by Jan Vishwas Act)

Exam Notes: Chapter XX – Penalties

Chapter XX details the penalties for various offenses under the Patents Act, 1970. 

  • Section 118: Contravention of secrecy provisions relating to certain inventions: Penalty for failing to comply with directions regarding inventions relevant to defense/atomic energy (Section 35) or filing abroad without following Section 39. Penalty includes imprisonment up to two years, or fine, or both.
  • Section 119: Falsification of entries in register, etc.: Penalty for making false entries or using false copies of entries in the patent register. Penalty includes imprisonment up to two years, or fine, or both.
  • Section 120: Unauthorised claim of patent rights: Penalty for falsely representing an article as patented or having a patent application pending in India. Penalty is a fine up to one lakh rupees.
  • Section 121: Wrongful use of words "patent office": Penalty for using "patent office" or similar terms to imply official connection with the Patent Office. Penalty includes imprisonment up to six months, or fine, or both.
  • Section 122: Refusal or failure to supply information: Penalty for refusing/failing to provide information requested by the government or Controller (Section 100(5), Section 146) (Subsection 1), or for providing false information (Subsection 2). Penalty under Subsection 1 is a fine up to ten lakh rupees. Penalty under Subsection 2 is imprisonment up to six months, or fine, or both.
  • Section 123: Practice by non-registered patent agents: Penalty for contravening restrictions on practicing as a patent agent without registration (Section 129). Penalty is a fine up to one lakh rupees for the first offense and five lakh rupees for subsequent offenses.
  • Section 124: Offences by companies: When a company commits an offense, both the company and individuals responsible for its business are liable, unless they prove lack of knowledge or due diligence. Directors, managers, etc., are also liable if the offense occurred with their consent or neglect.
