Q1. Designer Ltd. files an application for design registration in India on 15 March 2023, claiming priority from an application filed in the United Kingdom on 10 December 2022. The Indian application is identical to the UK application. Which of the following is correct?

(b) The registration is not invalidated solely because of such exhibition.
(c) The priority claim becomes void.
(d) The application must be refiled with a disclaimer.

Answer: (b) – Section 44(2) provides protection against invalidation due to exhibition/use/publication during the priority period.

3. Case Study: The Priority Conflict

Scenario: A German company, "Berlin Glass," applies for a design for a specialized lens in Germany on January 1, 2024. On March 1, 2024, they exhibit the lens at a trade fair in Mumbai. An Indian competitor, "IndiLens," sees the design and files an identical design application in India on March 15, 2024. "Berlin Glass" eventually files their Indian application on May 1, 2024, claiming priority from their German filing.

Analysis:

  1. Priority Right: Since Germany is a convention country, Berlin Glass has 6 months (until July 1, 2024) to file in India.
  2. Date of Registration: Berlin Glass’s Indian registration will be dated January 1, 2024.
  3. Outcome: Even though IndiLens filed before Berlin Glass's Indian application, Berlin Glass wins because their priority date is earlier.
  4. Piracy/Damages: Under Section 44(1)(b), Berlin Glass cannot sue IndiLens for damages for any sales IndiLens made between January and May, as damages are only recoverable after the actual registration date in India.

Use Case A: The Reciprocity Check

A defense contractor develops a new design for a tank tread that significantly reduces noise. They file for a design patent. The Controller will refuse to disclose the information to the public and may cancel the registration if directed by the Central Government, preventing the design from entering the public domain.

Q2: Who must receive the report? Answer: Both Houses of Parliament (Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha)

Q3: How frequently must this be done? Answer: Once a year (annual reporting)

📊 COMPREHENSIVE CASE STUDY

Case Study: International Priority Claim Gone Wrong

Background

GlobalDesigns Inc. is a US-based company that creates innovative consumer electronics. On December 1, 2023, they filed a design application for a revolutionary smartwatch design with the USPTO. The design featured unique aesthetics and ergonomic elements.

Timeline of Events

Date

Event

Dec 1, 2023

Filed design application in USA

Feb 15, 2024

Exhibited the smartwatch at Mobile World Congress in Barcelona

April 10, 2024

A competitor in India starts selling similar watches

May 20, 2024

Also filed in Germany (for EU protection)

July 15, 2024

Finally filed application in India claiming priority

Sept 1, 2024

Sept 5, 2024

Legal Issues & Analysis

ISSUE 1: Is the priority claim valid?

Analysis: The application in India was filed on July 15, 2024, which is more than 6 months from the US filing (Dec 1, 2023). The 6-month deadline was June 1, 2024. Therefore, priority claim is INVALID.

Relevant Section: Section 44(1)(a)

Conclusion: Priority claim will be rejected. The effective date will be July 15, 2024 (Indian filing date), not Dec 1, 2023.

ISSUE 2: Does the Barcelona exhibition matter?

Analysis: Section 44(2) protects against invalidation due to exhibition during the priority period. However, since priority claim is invalid, this protection may not apply effectively.

Relevant Section: Section 44(2)

Conclusion: Exhibition itself won't invalidate if filed within time, but here the main issue is late filing which negates priority benefits.

ISSUE 3: Can damages be recovered for April-August infringement?

Analysis: Section 44(1)(b) states proprietors cannot recover damages for piracy prior to actual registration date. Registration was on Sept 1, 2024.

Relevant Section: Section 44(1)(b)

Conclusion: NO damages recoverable for April-August period. Damages only from Sept 1, 2024 onwards.

ISSUE 4: What about the German filing?

Analysis: Multiple applications were filed (USA and Germany). Per Explanation 2, the period is calculated from the earliest application (USA - Dec 1). The German filing doesn't extend the deadline.

Relevant Section: Section 44, Explanation 2

Conclusion: German filing is irrelevant; deadline was already missed based on USA filing date.

Key Lessons Learned

  1. Strict Deadline Compliance: File within 6 months from the first convention country application - this is a strict, non-extendable deadline
  2. Damage Recovery Limitations: Priority date determines protection period but NOT damage recovery period in India
  3. Multiple Filing Strategy: Multiple convention filings don't extend the 6-month period; earliest date applies
  4. Timeline Management: Plan international filing strategy carefully with proper timeline management and calendar alerts
  5. Professional Assistance: Consider engaging local patent/design agents in target countries early in the process
  6. Documentation: Maintain certified copies and proof of all filing dates for priority claims
  7. Strategic Planning: Consider provisional design applications or staggered filing strategies for global protection
